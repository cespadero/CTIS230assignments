Below is the executive summary of Olay

Exective Summary


Olay, a skincare powerhouse that is an entity of Procter & Gamble (P&G), was founded in 1952. Originally called as Oil of Olay, the company acquired prominence for its distinctive oil-in-water emulsion mix, which distinguished it from the time's conventional skincare products. Olay has developed over the years into a major force in the worldwide beauty industry, known for its dedication to scientific advancement and provision of efficient skincare products. The company has successfully adapted to shifting customer preferences and beauty trends, positioning itself as a reliable partner in the quest for glowing, healthy skin and among its target audience. Its strategic marketing, inclusive approach, and dedication to quality have solidified Olay's position as a market leader, reflecting the brand's ability to adapt and thrive in the dynamic beauty industry.


Olay's worldwide presence, which includes operations in more than 150 countries, highlights its capacity to connect with a range of customer wants and satisfactions. The brand's success is due to both its consumer-centric culture and its ongoing dedication to innovation, which includes designing products to address a variety of skin issues. In line with Procter & Gamble's broad commitment to environmental stewardship, Olay's sustainability efforts further demonstrate its commitment to ethical beauty practices. Olay uses technology to interact with its audience and distinguish itself as a pioneer in the rapidly changing skincare industry in a time when digital engagement is crucial. Olay's mission is to empower individuals to embrace their best skin, offering a range of products that cater to diverse skin needs. Grounded in a philosophy that combines science and beauty, Olay is dedicated to fostering confidence and self-expression through skincare, aiming to be a trusted ally in every individual's unique beauty journey.Olay is a corporation supported by P&G's substantial resources, allowing for considerable investments in innovative research and development. A major asset of the company is its dedication to scientific innovation, which has resulted in the development of highly effective skincare products. Additionally, Olay has a competitive edge in the beauty sector due to its broad worldwide distribution and excellent brand awareness. Olay has a reputation for innovation, but a major vulnerability might be a lack of flexibility in responding to swiftly shifting customer demands. The company may struggle to meet the differing skincare demands of various ethnic groups, which might result in gaps in the product's applicability in some countries. Olay could grow by entering targeted marketing and e-commerce, the business may make use of digital channels, giving customers a smooth buying experience. Collaborations with influencers and beauty professionals offer opportunities to increase brand relevance and reach.

With fresh competitors and existing rivals constantly competing for consumers' attention, market competition poses a serious threat. Consumer expenditure on high-end skincare products may be impacted by economic downturns, necessitating creative pricing and marketing plans. Additionally, Olay must take proactive measures to overcome problems posed by changing regulatory environments, particularly with regard to ingredient limits.
